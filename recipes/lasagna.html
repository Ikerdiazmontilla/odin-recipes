Description

Indulge in layers of heavenly delight with this classic lasagna recipe. Perfectly seasoned meat sauce harmoniously blends with creamy bechamel and gooey mozzarella, all tucked between tender sheets of pasta. This lasagna is not just a dish, it's an experience. Ideal for family dinners, special occasions, or whenever you need a taste of comfort. Satisfy your Italian cravings and make room for seconds, because this lasagna is love at first bite!

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 can (6 oz) tomato paste
  • 1 tsp salt (for meat sauce)
  • 1 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • 4 cups whole milk
  • 4 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 4 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 tsp salt (for béchamel)
  • 12 lasagna noodles
  • 2 cups mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • 1 cup Parmesan cheese, grated
  • 1 tbsp olive oil for greasing

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven

    Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Prepare the meat sauce

    1. In a large skillet, brown the ground beef over medium heat.
    2. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and cook until the onions are translucent.
    3. Stir in the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, salt, black pepper, dried oregano, and dried basil.
    4. Simmer the sauce for about 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  3. Make the Béchamel Sauce

    1. In a separate saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat.
    2. Add the all-purpose flour, stirring continuously to form a roux.
    3. Gradually add the whole milk while continuing to stir, to prevent lumps from forming.
    4. Add the ground nutmeg and salt.
    5. Continue stirring until the sauce thickens.

  4. Cook the Lasagna Noodles

    Boil the lasagna noodles according to the package instructions, then drain and set aside.

  5. Grease the Baking Dish

    Lightly grease a 9x13-inch baking dish with olive oil.

  6. Assemble the Lasagna

    1. Place a layer of cooked lasagna noodles at the bottom of the baking dish.
    2. Spread a layer of meat sauce over the noodles.
    3. Pour a layer of béchamel sauce over the meat.
    4. Sprinkle a generous layer of shredded mozzarella and grated Parmesan cheese.
    5. Repeat the layers, ending with a layer of cheese on top.

  7. Bake the lasagna

    1. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake for 25 minutes.
    2. Remove the foil and continue to bake for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the cheese is golden and bubbly.

  8. Let it rest and serve

    • Once out of the oven, let the lasagna rest for 10 minutes before serving.

This breakdown should guide you through each phase of making your lasagna, making the process easier to manage. Enjoy your cooking!
