The best crema catalana

Description

Experience the sweetness of tradition with this classic recipe for a rich, aromatic dessert. Whether you like it extra sweet or moderately so, this creamy dish captures the essence of Spanish home cooking. Immerse yourself in the inviting aroma of orange and lemon peel, while hints of cinnamon take you on a sensory journey. It's more than just a dessert; it's an embrace of culture and warmth.

Ingredients

  • 1L of milk
  • 50-60g of flour, depending on desired density
  • 200g sugar (for very sweet) or 150g sugar (for regular sweetness)
  • 6 egg yolks
  • 1 orange
  • 1 lemon

Steps

  1. Prepare the Milk

    1. Pour 900ml of milk into a pot.
    2. Reserve 100ml of milk in a measuring cup.
    3. Heat the milk in the pot to 8 or 9 on the stove, ensuring it doesn't boil but remains as hot as possible without boiling.
    4. Add a cinnamon stick before the milk gets hot.
    5. Add the peel of an orange and a lemon, avoiding the white pith, using a peeler.
    6. Stir the milk occasionally and continue to heat to capture the aroma.

  2. Prepare the Sugar

    1. Measure either 200g of sugar for a very sweet dessert or 150g for a moderately sweet dessert.

  3. Mix Flour and Egg Yolks

    1. In the measuring cup with the reserved 100ml of milk, add the flour and stir until it is integrated and appears liquid-like.
    2. Separate the egg yolks and add them to the flour and milk mixture.
    3. Mix everything together.

  4. Combine All Ingredients

    1. Once the milk in the pot has heated for a while and captured the aroma, add the sugar.
    2. Use a strainer to remove any impurities, raise the heat slightly to maintain the temperature, and stir.
    3. Increase the heat until it is just about to boil. When the first bubbles appear, pour in the other mixture all at once while stirringâ€”first slowly, then more quickly as it starts to thicken.

  5. Finish and Cool

    1. Once the mixture has thickened, pour it into a container while it's still hot.
    2. Let it cool.
    3. Optional: Once cooled, you can make caramel and sprinkle it on top.
